Pixle? What is that?

Wordle, Picross and typical nonograms inspired the creation and development of the web-game Pixle. It combines these different ideas and improves certain aspects of its spiritual predecessors. The main goal of Pixle is to solve puzzles, or pixles as we like to call them. Pixles are nothing more than puzzles and follow a certain pattern. Its pieces consist of up to five different emojis.
Each day a new pixle reveals itself to the world, sometimes bringing players to its limits. To solve a pixle, the player has to place the emojis on their correct tiles or positions, shown to the player at the beginning of the game and after each failed try.

Wondering how Pixle works? No worries, we will explain thoroughly.
Below you can see a grid of symbols, or as we call them: "emojis". This is the area where the fun happens.

The grid represents a pixle which consists of up to five different emojis. You are shown the pixle once and as soon as its hidden, you have to use your memory to rebuild the pattern.
Doesn't seem too difficult, right?

Everytime you place an emoji incorrectly, you lose a try on the mismatched tile.
Don't worry, after each failed try, the pixle will flip and show the correct answers briefly.
But as soon as you run out of available tries, it's game over!

About puzzles and pixles

Puzzles, or pixles as we like to call them, are a fundamental part of the game Pixle. After all, the name "Pixle" derives from these different patterns.
But we don't want to talk all day long about this boring stuff. How are these pixles created? What is the process?
We built a small generator, which gets fed a few different predefined patterns and emojis. It chooses one random pattern out of the bunch and checks the amount of emojis needed to build this pattern. Once it knows the correct amount, it randomly picks various emojis out of the list and makes sure double usage won't happen. An emoji can only be used once in each pattern.
After choosing a pattern and picking some emojis, both get thrown together and stored in an array. A new pixle is born! Along with the pixle, other important information is stored in the array, such as the pixles id and publication date.
And that should cover everything. Now you really now everything about the puzzles, pixles and the game Pixle.
